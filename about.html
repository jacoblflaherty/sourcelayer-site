About

Jake Flaherty

Technical Implementation & Systems Operator. CRM/ATS workflows, Google Workspace, customer adoption, and practical process improvement. I turn messy operations into usable systems.

Why Source Layer exists

Source Layer was built for successful businesses whose backend systems have become harder to manage than they should be.

Most operational problems don't start because the team is careless or the business is broken. They start because the company grows: new tools get added, workflows change, folders multiply, permissions get messy, and software gets used in ways no one ever properly designed. Eventually the business has the tools — but not the structure.

That's where Source Layer comes in.

How I ended up doing this

My name is Jake, and I've spent 12+ years across sales, sales leadership, product, staffing operations, business development, workflow improvement, and client-facing systems work. I didn't come into this from theory. I came into it by helping solve a real operational mess.

A staffing agency owner I knew had purchased Bullhorn, but the implementation had ended with gaps — thin training, a weak handoff, very little practical guidance. I offered to take a look. That turned into a deeper cleanup project. The system was technically in place, but it wasn't helping the business run better: records were misused, data was messy, follow-up visibility was weak, workflows were unclear. The team had software, but not a usable operating system.

So I learned the system, worked through documentation and support resources, mapped how the business actually operated, and started turning the mess into something the team could use. That experience became the foundation for Source Layer.

What the work looks like

Today, Source Layer helps owner-led businesses clean up the messy backend systems that slow their teams down — from Google Workspace, admin workflows, folders, permissions, templates, and SOPs to Bullhorn and recruiting operations.

The work is practical. We look at how your business actually runs, where things are getting stuck, what your team is avoiding, and what needs to be simplified. Then we clean up the structure, clarify the workflows, and make the tools easier to trust and use.

I'm not here to sell complexity back to you. I'm here to help you understand what's broken, what matters, what can be ignored, and what needs to be fixed first. The goal is simple: get you and your team out of the weeds so the business can run with less confusion, less rework, and less dependence on messy workarounds.

How I work
