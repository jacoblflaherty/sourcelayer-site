The real question

Bullhorn was installed. But was it actually implemented?

You got the software, the logins, and a basic setup. The deeper work — workflow design, data structure, reporting, and operating standards — is often a separate, five-figure project. When that part doesn’t happen, the team quietly fills the gaps itself.

One recruiter uses “Screening” to mean a brand-new candidate; another uses it to mean someone they’ve already contacted. Someone keeps a private spreadsheet of their best people. When the owner asks for pipeline numbers, somebody rebuilds them by hand the night before. Candidates sit in the database, but recruiters still work from the names they remember. Old placements stay marked placed long after the assignment ended. And nobody is quite sure what Bullhorn support is supposed to fix.

If that sounds familiar, the check will show where the implementation is most likely unfinished.
