The situation

Sound familiar?

  • Bullhorn was set up years ago by someone who's gone, and nobody fully knows how it's configured
  • Recruiters keep their real pipeline in spreadsheets, inboxes, and memory — not in the system
  • Duplicate candidates, stale contacts, and half-filled records make every search a gamble
  • Leadership can't pull a report they trust, so decisions run on gut feel
  • Sales and recruiting each use the system their own way, and the handoffs leak
  • You're paying for Bullhorn every month, and it feels like overhead instead of an advantage

None of this means your team failed. It usually means the implementation stopped before the system was actually finished — no cleanup, no standards, no training, no adoption plan. That's the gap I fill.

The reframe

Finding the problems isn't bad news. It's the good news.

Your business already works — you're placing candidates and billing clients despite the mess. Every point of friction we find in your system is drag you've been paying for without knowing it. Once it's named, it can be fixed. That's when Bullhorn stops being overhead and starts carrying its weight.

What I do

Clean it up. Make it stick. Keep it working.

Clean it up

Records, duplicates, fields, stages, statuses, and account structure — reviewed, rationalized, and cleaned so searches work and data means something again.

Keep it working
