How I ended up doing this

I got here the practical way: sitting inside a staffing agency's Bullhorn environment that had never been properly finished, and fixing it piece by piece while the business kept running. That work — the record cleanup, the workflow repair, the documentation, the patient conversations with people who'd given up on the system — turned out to be a repeatable skill that most agencies need and almost nobody sells.

The same pattern shows up outside staffing: owner-led businesses running the whole company through Gmail, Drive, and spreadsheets that grew instead of being structured. Different tools, same fix — cleanup, standards, training, and follow-through until the habits change.

How I work

Five working principles

  • Operator first I do the work in the system, not just write recommendations about it.
  • Fixed scope over open meters You should know what a project costs before it starts.
  • Plain language If a recruiter can't understand the new standard in one read, the standard is wrong.
  • Adoption is the deliverable A clean database nobody uses is just a tidier failure. The job isn't done until the team's habits change.
  • Practical AI, sparingly I use AI tooling where it genuinely speeds up cleanup and documentation work — and nowhere it doesn't.

What I'm not

I'm not a Bullhorn employee, partner, or certified reseller — I'm an independent operator who works on your side of the table. I'm not a strategy firm, and I won't hand you a deck where a fix should be. And I'm not tech support or a software tutor — I fix the structure, then teach your team to run it.
