Inside the sprint

Three phases, in order

01

The audit

Every sprint starts with the fixed-fee audit. I go through your Bullhorn environment and map what's actually there:

  • Data health — duplicates, stale records, incomplete candidate and contact data
  • Structure — how leads, contacts, companies, and jobs are actually organized, versus how they should be
  • Fields, stages, and statuses — what exists, what's used, what's noise
  • Workflows — where the recruiting and sales process breaks inside the system
  • Usage — how each role actually works today, and where the system gets bypassed
  • Reporting — why leadership can't get numbers they trust, and what has to change first

If you stop there, you still have a map any competent admin could execute. Most clients continue into the sprint.

02

The cleanup

The sprint executes the audit's priority list. Depending on what your system needs, that typically includes:

  • Duplicate and inactive record review and cleanup
  • Candidate and contact record cleanup and re-categorization
  • Lead / contact / account structure repair
  • Field, stage, and status rationalization — fewer, clearer, actually used
  • Workflow repair so the system matches how your team really sells and recruits
  • CRM/ATS usage standards: who enters what, when, and how
03

Documentation & handoff

Cleanup without adoption is temporary. The sprint ends with:

  • Written usage standards in plain language — not a binder nobody opens
  • Role-based walkthroughs for recruiters, salespeople, and admins
  • A handoff session with whoever owns the system internally
